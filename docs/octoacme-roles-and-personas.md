# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## UX Designer

### Role Summary
UX Designers design user interfaces and experiences grounded in user research. They ensure that product decisions are informed by real user needs and that delivered features are accessible and usable.

### Responsibilities
- Develop wireframes, mockups, and interactive prototypes
- Conduct usability testing and synthesize user research findings
- Collaborate with Product Managers on acceptance criteria and feature specs
- Work with Developers to ensure faithful implementation of designs
- Establish and maintain accessibility standards across the product

### Goals
- Ensure high usability, accessibility, and positive user sentiment
- Reduce user friction and support faster onboarding
- Advocate for user needs throughout the product lifecycle

### Typical Communication
- Design reviews with Product Managers and Developers
- Usability test reports and design documentation
- Participation in sprint planning to clarify design intent

---

## DevOps Engineer

### Role Summary
DevOps Engineers build and maintain CI/CD pipelines, automation, and deployment infrastructure. They bridge development and operations to enable frequent, reliable software releases.

### Responsibilities
- Configure and maintain CI/CD pipelines and deployment automation
- Monitor deployments and production environments for stability
- Automate testing environments and infrastructure provisioning
- Handle incident response infrastructure and runbooks
- Collaborate on security and compliance requirements for infrastructure

### Goals
- Improve deployment frequency and reduce time-to-recovery
- Ensure infrastructure reliability and observability
- Reduce manual toil through automation

### Typical Communication
- Coordination with Developers during feature rollouts
- Environment status updates with QA for stability assurance
- Incident post-mortems and infrastructure change documentation

---

## Scrum Master

### Role Summary
Scrum Masters facilitate agile ceremonies, help the team improve velocity, and remove process blockers. They act as servant-leaders, enabling the team to self-organize and continuously improve.

### Responsibilities
- Facilitate daily standups, sprint planning, reviews, and retrospectives
- Identify and remove impediments that block team progress
- Support the team in following agile principles and practices
- Track team metrics (velocity, cycle time) and surface trends
- Shield the team from external disruptions during sprints

### Goals
- Achieve high and consistent team velocity
- Ensure adherence to agile principles and continuous process improvement
- Foster a culture of transparency, collaboration, and accountability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and retrospective facilitation
- Impediment logs and sprint metrics reports
- Close collaboration with Product Managers and Developers on delivery cadence

---

## Business Analyst

### Role Summary
Business Analysts drive requirements gathering and translate business needs into actionable user stories. They ensure alignment between stakeholder expectations and technical delivery.

### Responsibilities
- Elicit, document, and validate requirements from stakeholders
- Clarify and refine acceptance criteria for user stories
- Facilitate alignment sessions between technical and business teams
- Identify gaps or ambiguities in requirements and resolve them proactively
- Maintain traceability between business goals and delivered features

### Goals
- Produce clear, actionable requirements that development teams can implement confidently
- Ensure alignment between business goals and technical delivery
- Reduce rework caused by misunderstood or incomplete requirements

### Typical Communication
- Requirements workshops and stakeholder interviews
- Collaboration with Product Managers, Developers, and Stakeholders
- Acceptance criteria documentation and backlog refinement sessions
